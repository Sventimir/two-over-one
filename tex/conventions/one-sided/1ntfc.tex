\subsection{Forcing 1\nt}
\label{subsec:1nt-fc}

\conv{Forcing \ctr{1\nt}} in response to \hyperref[subsec:1maj-op]{\ctr{1\major} opening} includes three possible types
of~hands:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \so\ \hcp{3}{6} \ws{}
  \item \inv\ \hcp{10}{12} \ws{}
  \item any \so\/ or \inv\/ hand \wos{}
\end{itemize}

Basically it means any hand under \gf\/ strength excluding \hcp{7}{9} \ws{}. After \ctr{1\h} opening it also excludes
\suit{4+}{\s}. \pagebreak[2]

\emph{Opener}\/ has to respond, giving a more deatiled description of his hand:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \ctr{2\c} -- \suit{4+}{\c} or \bal\ (after \ctr{1\h} opening \suit{4}{\s} is also possible with minimum strength)
  \item \ctr{2\d/\h} (if below \opn's suit) -- \suit{4+}{\d/\h}
  \item \ctr{2 \opn's suit} -- \hcp{11}{13} \suit{6+}{\major}
  \item \ctr{2\s} (\om) -- \rev\ \suit{4}{\s}
  \item \ctr{2\nt} -- \hcp{18}{19} \bal
  \item \ctr{3\minor/\h} -- \rev\ \suit{4+}{\minor}
  \item \ctr{3 \opn's suit} -- \hcp{14}{17} \suit{6+}{\major}
  \item \ctr{3\s} (\om) -- \conv{autopslinter} \question
\end{itemize}

Further bidding is natural except for the \hyperref[subsec:bart]{\conv{Bart}} convetion. \emph{Responder's}
rebid in \opn's suit declares at least tollerance in that suit, \textit{i.e.} \suit{3+}{} cards and weak hand or
doubleton with somewhat greater strength (although it's still \so). Jump rebid in \opn's suit is \inv\ \ws{}.

Bidding by the \resp\/ a new suit below 2 in \opn's suit is \nat\ \so, while a new suit above 2 in \opn's suit is
\nat\ \inv\/ (at least \suit{5+}{cards}).

\subsubsection{Bart}
\label{subsec:bart}

\sequence{1\s\ --- 1\nt\ --- 2\c\ --- ?}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \ctr{2\d} -- ambiguous:
    \begin{itemize}
      \item \suit{5+}{\h}, \suit{2}{\s}, weak (will pass any rebid)
      \item 9--11, Hx \s\ (will bid or pass \ctr{2\s} or \emph{invite} in \nt)
      \item \inv\ in \d\ (will rebid \ctr{3\d})
      \item \so\ \ws{\c} (will rebid \ctr{3\c})
      \item \inv\ \ws{\c} (will rebid \ctr{2NT})
    \end{itemize}
  \item \ctr{2\h} -- $6+$ (or SOL $5$) \h, singleton \s, \so
  \item \ctr{2\s} -- weak \ws{} or 6--9 \wos{}
  \item \ctr{2\nt} -- \inv\ \wos{\c}
  \item \ctr{3\minor/\h} -- preemption
\end{itemize}
\vspace{1em}

\sequence{1\s\ --- 1\nt\ --- 2\c\ --- 2\d\ --- ?}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \ctr{2\h} -- \so\ \ws{\h}
  \item \ctr{2\s} -- \so\ \wos{\h}
  \item \ctr{2\nt} -- strength for game, worry about \d or \h
  \item \ctr{3\c} -- \gf, \suit{5+}{\s} \& \suit{5+}{\c}
  \item \ctr{3\d} -- \gf, \suit{5}{\s}-\suit{4}{\c}-\suit{3\hyp 4}{\d}-\suit{0\hyp 1}{\c}
  \item \ctr{3\h} -- \gf\ \ws{\h}
  \item \ctr{3\s} -- \gf, \suit{5\hyp 6}{\s}, \suit{4\hyp 5}{\c}
\end{itemize}
